Introduction

Not only is the Book of Mormon written in a style that imitates the King James Bible, but it also incorporates actual phrases and sometimes entire blocks of material from the KJV. Many Book of Mormon passages combine phrases from several different biblical texts. The more interesting quotations are those that are taken from the New Testament, long before the biblical documents were even composed. The sheer volume of these quotations also has serious implications for such issues as the manner in which the Book of Mormon was supposedly translated. The following material lists some of the biblical texts found in the Book of Mormon. This list is in no way complete, but it illustrates how biblical text is literally woven into the Book of Mormon.

The Book of Mormon also employs some favorite phrases with high frequency. For example, in the entire Bible, including both Old and New Testaments and the Apocrypha, phrases with "came to pass" occur 480 times, but 1,355 times in the Book of Mormon. The phrases "and now" and "and now, behold" occur 160 times in the Bible, but 650 times in the Book of Mormon. The phrase "face of the earth" occurs 33 times in the Bible and 49 times in the Book of Mormon. The Book of Mormon adds another 82 occurrences of "face of the land" and "face of this land," which do not appear in the Bible at all. Phrases with "depths of" occur 8 times in the Bible and 27 times in the Book of Mormon. The phrase "all manner of" occurs 47 times in the Bible and 110 times in the Book of Mormon. And it must be remembered that the Book of Mormon is only about one-third the size of the Bible.

It is evident that a very large portion of the words in the Book of Mormon come from the Bible, both Old and New Testaments.
