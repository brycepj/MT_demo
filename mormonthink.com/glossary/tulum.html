Tulum

Situated on the Yucatan Penninsula, Tulum is a Pre-Columbian city. It is often included in LDS tour packages as a Book of Mormon site - sites believed to be connected somehow with places mentioned in that book. Archaeologists have long known about Tulum and there has been much legitimate research of the area carried out by professional archaeologists. "All structural and ceramic evidence at Tulúm, as well as its important corpus of murals and relief sculpture, date from the Middle and Late Postclassic (AD 1200-1520)." (Athena Review Vol.2, no.1, "Maya sites in Quintana Roo: Tulúm" http://www.athenapub.com/tulumint.htm)

Sometimes tours try to convince people that the city is older than it really is, playing up the link to the ancient inhabitants mentioned in the Book of Mormon by trying to tie-in an old stele found there. However, archaeologists say that "Stela 1, found at Tulúm by Stephens and Catherwood in 1843 with an Early Classic date of 9.6.10.0.0 (AD 564), probably came to Tulúm from nearby Xelhá. Another reused stela of AD 761 (9.16.10.0.0) originated only a few km to the north at Tancah..." ("Maya sites in Quintana Roo: Tulúm" http://www.athenapub.com/tulumint.htm)

One of the more "important" items in Tulum for tourists is the "Diving God," or "Descending God." Tour groups catering to LDS clientele often try and tell that is represents Jesus Christ visiting the Book of Mormon people. However, The Ancient History Encyclopedia has this to say:

While it has long been held that Tulum is the only temple complex to depict the Descending God, his image has been found elsewhere. Attempts to link him to the figure of Jesus Christ have been dismissed by all reputable scholarly authorities.

http://www.ancient.eu.com/image/756/

In addition, archaeologists believe that the Descending God

...also known at Cobá and Sayil, are commonly thought, based on their apparent antennae and insect-like torsos, to represent the bee gods Ah Muzencab, known from the Madrid Codex.

("Maya sites in Quintana Roo: Tulúm" http://www.athenapub.com/tulumint.htm)

Another claim made by these touring sites is that Joseph Smith "identified the ancient name of the city of Zion as being Zama" and they say that the Tulum's original name was Zama. However, there are no recorded instances of Joseph Smith making the connection between "Zama" and "Zion." It is not clear where this rumor started, but it wouldn't be surprising if it was a tour guide looking for an extra tip at the hands of an unsuspecting Mormon tourist.

Most of the sites promoting Tulum as a Book of Mormon site often make grand pronouncements, but they do not have research backing up their claims. An example: http://www.yucatan-revealed.com/Christ-In-Tulum.html
